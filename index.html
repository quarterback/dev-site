Ron Bronson

Finds messy systems. Makes them legible. Builds what’s missing. Keeps them moving.

I work on how AI and automation reshape the systems people depend on — especially where service, judgment, trust, and accountability collide.

Increasingly that means directing the AI itself. I orchestrate fleets of agents from vague intent to shipped, working software — as a design leader, not an engineer, which is the point: the scarce skill isn’t typing, it’s decomposing ambiguous goals, keeping agents on-spec when they drift, designing the evals that tell you the output is actually right, and gating quality so it ships. Recently that’s been five production systems built and deployed in about five months — me as architect, product owner, and the human-in-the-loop gate; the agents did the typing.
