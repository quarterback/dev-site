Leafroll

A protocol-native professional network built on ATProto. Leafroll replaces connection collecting with intentional, bilateral relationships and portable identity.

The premise Professional networking with intention. Earned relationships, not an ever-growing contact list nobody really knows.

What it does

The dominant professional networks reward accumulation: connect with everyone, broadcast to everyone, measure your worth by a number that only goes up. Leafroll starts from the opposite assumption — that the relationships worth having are earned, mutual, and finite.

  • Bilateral by design. A connection means something because both sides chose it. No one-way follows that masquerade as relationships.
  • Intentional, not infinite. Reaching out costs something, so outreach carries signal instead of noise — the same logic that makes a recommendation worth more than a mass invite.
  • Portable identity. Your identity and data live with you on ATProto, not inside the platform. Leafroll is one application you use, not a silo you’re locked into.
  • Portable trust. Reputation can travel with you across protocol-native apps rather than resetting every time you join a new one.

Why ATProto

Professional networks are the clearest case of platform lock-in: your reputation, your relationships, and your history are held hostage by a single company. Building on the AT Protocol flips that. Identity is portable, the data is yours, and the network is an application on shared infrastructure rather than a walled garden. That’s what makes “earned, bilateral relationships” more than a slogan — the protocol is what lets trust and identity actually move with the person.

Part of a larger body of work

Leafroll is one of several protocol-native experiments, each rethinking a familiar category for a world of portable identity. It shares its trust model with Glowrm and sits alongside PBJ, Live Audio, and other ATProto work.
