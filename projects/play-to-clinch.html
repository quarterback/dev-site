Overview

College tennis is a strange, under-modeled sport. A dual match is one doubles point plus six singles courts, and the whole thing ends the moment one side reaches the clinching point — so matches stop mid-rally, lineup order becomes a chess match between coaches, and a single court can decide everything. Play To Clinch takes that format seriously and turns it into something you can actually manage.

You run the program end to end: recruit and develop players, weigh singles strength against doubles chemistry, set your lineup card before each dual, and live with how it plays out across a season toward the conference tournament. The name is the format — you don’t play out every point, you play to the clinch.

As far as I can tell, it’s the first college tennis management sim anywhere — the kind of niche, lovingly specific system that only exists if someone decides to build it. That’s the through-line in most of what I make: find a corner of the world no one has modeled, and build the working instrument that lets you actually play with how it behaves.
